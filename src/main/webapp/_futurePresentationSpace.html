Pedagogicometry

This is the mechanism which enables a user to tailor their own experience to the capabilities of their device and curriculum needs. A confident student might want to annotate immediately, whereas a shy student might prefer not to know that anyone else is doing so, and to confine themselves to structured responses (quizzes and multiple choices and so forth). Some students might even prefer to follow the teacher's 'camera' directly, seeing only the content which is didactically emphasised.
The specific toolset presented in this technical demonstration comes in two parts:
  • A row of buttons, enabling the selection of pedagogical levels of engagement.

    This can be found at the top left of the MeTL interface. Available levels are:
    1. The user cannot move their camera, and cannot add any content in any way. Polls cannot be seen or responded to. This is a purely one-way interaction.
    2. The user can move their camera, finding content of interest across the presentation space and following HUD groups.
    3. The user can move their camera and can also access and respond to polls. This is the basic level of a dynamic Powerpoint presentation.
    4. The user can add content freely and can edit their own content. They can respond in both low and high fidelity media, and can also mark the presentation content up privately for later revision.
    5. The user can add content in any way they choose, and can also form groups. These groups can collaborate across MeTL content as well as web-available content. They may attempt assessment events together (formal assessments impose rules about joining and leaving the groups). Students seeking groups can see how long the group has been in existence as well as what it is trying to achieve.
  • A set of configuration toggles, found under the Options dialog (the ellipsis under the MeTL button).

    These allow the user to specifically tailor their experience to the capabilities of their device - a user without a multitouch screen might, for instance, need to enable explicit Panning and Zooming modes irrespective of their pedagogical level. A user on an iPad might prefer to emphasize inking over text, and a user on a Mac might prefer the opposite.
